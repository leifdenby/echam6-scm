Fall 1997 Single Column Model (SCM) IOP

Seasonal SCM IOPs are conducted at the Southern Great Plains to enhance the frequency of observations for SCM uses, particularly vertical soundings of temperature, water vapor, and winds. The SCM IOPs are conducted for a period of 21 days. During that time, radiosondes are launched at the Central Facility and the four boundary facilities eight times per day, seven days per week. The data are required for quantifying boundary forcing and column response.

Model results are available for the Fall 1997 SCM IOP.

Description:

Analysis for the ARM Sept. 1997 IOP data: The analysis is based on the variational method described in Zhang and Lin (1997) and Zhang et al. (2001). The following features are included in the implementation version (IV-1), used in this analysis.

(1) Hourly wind data from 17 profilers surrounding the ARM CART array have been used as additional input.

(2) A scale-controlled statistical interpolation procedure similar to Ooyama (1987) has been implemented in our pre-processing step.

(3) All upper-air data have been smoothed by using a three-point running mean in time as an attempt to de-aliase unresolvable features. All surface variables are smoothed accordingly.

(4) The error variance functions in the variational procedures vary with height according to estimation of both instrument and sampling uncertainties.

(5) More rigorous quality control procedures have been implemented to screen raw sounding and profiler data.

(6) All constraint variables are derived by first gridding them and then taking the area average. Measurements from a variety of platforms (SMOS, EBBR, SONDES, Ok Mesonet, Kansan Mesonet, ...) are merged to produce the gridded surface composites. Latent and sensible heat fluxes are obtained from the EBBR stations

***Note: Radar rainfall was not used, for precipitation estimation, for this IOP.

There are two fortran source code files to read the data (for single-level fields and for multi-layer fields). Everything is self-explained in the source files. A list of the single-level fields and multi-layer fields in the data files is attached here. I have included fields in addition to those for running SCMs in the data files for your diagnostic purpose.

More detailed information about the variational analysis method can be found in Zhang and Lin (1997) and Zhang et al. (2001)

Reference:

Zhang, M.H. and J.L. Lin, 1997: Constrained variational analysis of sounding data based on column-integrated conservations of mass, heat, moisture, and momentum: Approach and application to ARM measurements. J. Atmos. Sci., 54, 1503-1524.

Zhang, M.H., J.L. Lin, R.T. Cederwall, J.J. Yio, and S.C. Xie, 2001: Objective analysis of ARM IOP data: Method and sensitivity. Mon. Wea. Rev., 129, 295-311.
